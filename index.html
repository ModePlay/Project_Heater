Glute Bridge

Lie down on your back with knees bent and feet on the mat. Tighten your core and then press into heels and squeeze the glutes to raise your hips upwards, while pushing the upper back into the floor. Hold this position and then return to start to complete 1 rep.

Super Girl

All fours with knees under hips and hands under shoulders. Bring your left elbow to meet your right knee underneath your torso. Then extend both your arm out and leg back parallel with the floor (fly like Super Girl!) and hold for a second. Once all reps are completed, repeat same on opposite sides.

*Is it a bird? Is it a plane?*

Draw Circles

Stand up straight arms out to the side like a "T." Keep arms straight the entire time. With your fingertips, draw the tiniest circles in the air as you can, gradually increasing the size until the circles are as big as you can draw, then gradually reduce the size. When the circles are once again small as can be, Repeat in the reverse direction. Take a break and do it all one more time.

*Feel the burn!*

Reverse Flys

5lbs/arm is plenty to start. Or do it without weight at first to get a feel. Stand with knees slightly bent. Bend at waist so torso is parallel to the ground as can be. Chin up and head forward. Keeping arms straight with a slight bend in your elbows, flair your arms up and out to the sides squeezing your shoulders at the top--that's where you want focus. DON'T try and hold the weight up at the top of the lift. Should be smooth and fluid reps almost like you're flapping wings.

*Kaw-kaww*

Box Lift

Safest thing I can think of to target your lower back. Legs slightly more than shoulder-width apart. Arms above your head and imagine a box in your hands. Bend at the waist (push butt out) only so far as keeping your back and pelvis aligned will allow, then let your arms drop further to place the 'box' at your feet. Then push your hips forward as you pick the 'box' back up and overhead. Your legs should not move at all.

*Imagination is a powerful thing*

Kickbacks

Start with about 2 1/2lbs per hand. Stand up with slight bend in knees. Bend forward at waist. Chin up and head forward. Pull elbows up so tris are parallel to the ground as you can make them and hold them there (you should almost look like a downhill skiier). Now your reps begin by extending BOTH your forearms out toward the back. Or to say another way, the front of your fist will point from the ground to the back wall for one rep.

*The further forward you safely lean, the more it will hit your tris*

90's Hold

Not as hard as plank. Lay on your back. Tuck your tailbone so there is no gap between your back and the floor. Bring your knees up and make calves parallel to the floor so your legs make a 90 deg angle and hold. The trick is to also press your back into the mat and not let it arch back off or alternatively can think of it as maintaining your pelvic tilt.

*Chill out. Max out. Relax all cool....*
